GOAL 2 : ZERO HUNGER

Why Zero Hunger Matters

Goal 2 is dedicated to ending hunger in all its forms by ensuring that every individual — especially children, women, and vulnerable populations — has reliable access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food throughout the year. Beyond simply providing food, this goal also promotes sustainable agricultural practices, supports small-scale farmers, and encourages responsible food production that protects natural resources, soil health, water supplies, and biodiversity.

  • Ensure year-round access to safe and nutritious food for everyone
  • Support small-scale farmers and increase their productivity and income
  • Promote sustainable and climate-resilient agricultural practices
  • Protect ecosystems, biodiversity, soil, and water resources
  • Reduce food waste and improve food distribution systems

By 2030, this goal focuses on eliminating all forms of malnutrition, improving access to nutritious food for all, and doubling the productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers. At the same time, it emphasizes building sustainable, climate-resilient food systems.

Promote Sustainable Food

Sustainable food systems protect the environment while ensuring long-term food security. Supporting local farmers, adopting eco-friendly practices, and encouraging community-based agricultural programs are essential. By integrating innovative techniques such as crop rotation, agroforestry, and organic farming, we can reduce environmental impact while producing healthy food.

  • Organic farming practices for healthier soil
  • Support local farmers and co-ops
  • Conserve natural resources like water and biodiversity
  • Adopt climate-smart agriculture practices
  • Encourage community gardens and urban farming

Promoting sustainable food ensures that future generations have access to nutritious food while maintaining a balanced ecosystem.

Reduce Food Waste

Reducing food waste is critical to feeding more people and protecting the environment. By planning meals, storing food properly, and composting organic waste, households and businesses can significantly lower the amount of food that goes to landfills. Additionally, redistributing surplus food to those in need is an effective way to combat hunger.

  • Plan meals carefully to avoid excess
  • Properly store fruits, vegetables, and grains
  • Compost organic waste to enrich soil
  • Donate surplus food to local communities
  • Educate others on reducing food waste

Every small step contributes to a more sustainable food system and helps ensure that no one goes hungry.

Expanding Zero Hunger Initiatives

Hunger and malnutrition remain pressing challenges worldwide. Millions of people, particularly children, women, and vulnerable populations, are affected. To achieve SDG Goal 2, we need a multi-layered approach that combines policy, community programs, and individual actions.

9% of the world's population

are chronically undernourished, highlighting the need for urgent interventions.

Over 30% of food

produced globally is lost or wasted, emphasizing the importance of proper storage and redistribution.

Small-scale farmers

produce 70% of the world’s food; supporting them ensures sustainable agricultural development.

How You Can Help

  • Support local farmers and sustainable food businesses.
  • Reduce personal food waste by planning meals and proper storage.
  • Participate in community gardens or food donation programs.
  • Educate others about the importance of nutrition and sustainable practices.
  • Advocate for policies that improve food security and access.
